    Who Are the Winners and Losers of Each Round in Record of Ragnarok?

    Record of Ragnarok is a violent contest of 1-on-1 fights between some pretty fantastic and crazy matchups. The tournament, Ragnarok, is won by the first team to reach 7 victories. You’ll see Adam from the biblical creation story pitted against Zeus from Greek myth, but also more grounded characters like Jack the Ripper, but don’t underestimate any of the contenders.

    There have been enormous victories for humanity despite the odds seemingly stacked against them, as listed below for each round:

    • Round 1: Thor (W, Gods) vs. Lü Bu (L, Humans)
    • Round 2: Zeus (W, Gods) vs. Adam (L, Humans)
    • Round 3: Poseidon (L, Gods) vs. Kojiro Sasaki (W, Humans)
    • Round 4: Heracles (L, Gods) vs. Jack the Ripper (W, Humans)
    • Round 5: Shiva (W, Gods) vs. Raiden Tameemon (L, Humans)
    • Round 6: Zerofuku/Hajun (L, Gods) vs. Buddha (W, Humans)
    • Round 7: Hades (L, Gods) vs. Qin Shi Huang (W, Humans)
    • Round 8: Beelzebub (W, Gods) vs. Nikola Tesla (L, Humans)
    • Round 9: Apollo (W, Gods) vs. Leonidas (L, Humans)
    • Round 10: Susano’o no Mikoto vs. Soji Okita (Ongoing)

    Related: The 12 Strongest Characters in Record of Ragnarok

    Who is Fighting in Round 10?

    With more losers than winners for humanity in Record of Ragnarok, we’re seeing Round 10 feature humanity’s Soji Okita against the god Susano’o no Mikoto. Soji is pointed out to be humanity’s greatest “manslayer” while Susano’o no Mikoto is the “god slayer” of the heavens, and it promises to be the first truly epic sword duel of the series.

    Soji Okita fans might recognize he is a character lifted directly by Shinya Umemura (one of the creators of Record of Ragnarok) out of another series of his, Chiruran: Shinsengumi Requiem.

    Humanity is now trailing the Gods 4-5 after a string of disappointments. There are still a few tricks each team has up its sleeves, with the most recent fight being a grudge match between a classic Greek god against a Spartan legend. Leonidas’ resentment made him a fierce opponent, but Apollo’s grace and surprising humanism won even the hardened warrior over, winning beautifully as shown in Chapter 83.
