    In FIFA 23, and in soccer in general, knowing how to adapt is a must for any attacker, as being able to dribble past defenders can be the decisive factor between scoring a hat trick or ending the game at zero. With that said, very few moves are both as simple and as vital as the step-over, which became famous thanks to Brazilian legends such as Ronaldo and is a great tool for fooling defenders. But how can you perform a step over on FIFA 23?

    How to Do a Step Over in FIFA 23

    Currently, you can perform two different types of stopovers on FIFA 23: the standard step over, which can be performed by tilting the right trigger downward either clockwise (Right Stepover) or counterclockwise (Left Stepover) at a 45-degree angle. There is also the reverse step over, which can be performed by tilting the right trigger upward on an anticlockwise (Reverse Stepover right) or clockwise angle (reverse stepover left).

    To recap, here’s how to perform all stepovers on the game:

    • Stepover (Right): Tilt the right trigger downward clockwise while in possession of the ball.
    • Stepover (Left): Tilt the right trigger downward counterclockwise while in possession of the ball.
    • Reverse Stepover (Right): Tilt the right trigger upward clockwise while in possession of the ball.
    • Reverse Stepover (Left): Tilt the right trigger upward counterclockwise while in possession of the ball.

    Is important to point out that the step over is a 2-star move, so make sure your desired player is capable of performing it before attempting it on a real match.
