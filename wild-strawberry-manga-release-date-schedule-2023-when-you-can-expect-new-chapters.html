    What is Wild Strawberry About?

    Wild Strawberry introduces us to an alternate version of Tokyo, Japan, that has been taken over by evolved flowers that can consume humans to become powerful monsters called Jinka. Because of these new monsters, a force called the Flower Funeral Force eradicates Jinka and anyone that attempts to harbor them. The manga follows two protagonists, Kingo and Kayano, who do their best to survive, but when Kayano becomes a Jinka, Kingo will do anything he can to get her back!

    Wild Strawberry Early Impressions

    I found Wild Strawberry quite different from what I anticipated based on the description provided in the series announcement by Shueisha. It is a graphic and violent manga that doesn’t shy away from depicting the harsh reality of a parasitic invasion. The genre of this manga reminds me of Chainsaw Man, After God, and Jigokurak, where the world’s dystopian state is portrayed uniquely, which most manga series can’t do effectively. The artwork of Wild Strawberry is exceptional, and the storyline is straightforward to understand, which complements the artwork perfectly. I am incredibly excited to see what happens in the upcoming chapters of this new and genuinely terrifying manga series.
