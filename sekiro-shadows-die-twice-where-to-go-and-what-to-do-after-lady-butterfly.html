    Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice has a bit of an odd pacing structure for an action game. With most other titles in the genre, players are funneled down a path. Even if it has some open elements or areas, everything trends back toward the main path, with no major deviations allowed. That’s not the case here, and with some areas it’s unclear what to do after you complete them. To help, here’s where to go and what to do after beating Lady Butterfly in Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice.

    Where to go and What to do After Lady Butterfly

    First off, if you ended up here before you beat Lady Butterfly, be sure to do that using the guide linked there. The interesting thing about what to do after is that the answer is actually a bit different for everyone. This is because Lady Butterfly acts as the final boss for the Hirata Estate area, which acts almost solely as a side adventure off the main quest.

    Do you need to play through it? Yes, it has powerups, items, and upgrades that you certainly need to beat the game. But Lady Butterfly stands at the end of all of that, not actually blocking access to them. Everything you require from Hirata Estate is out in the game world. What beating her does is give you additional upgrades that will be a big help, but aren’t absolutely necessary.

    And what this means is that what to do after Lady Butterfly is simply to return back to whatever other part of the game you were working on. Have you beaten Gyoubu yet? If not, that’s your next step. Genichiro is likely also someone you need to face off against. Basically the answer to where to go and what to do after beating Lady Butterfly in Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice is to just go back to wherever you were before. Beating her completes the mission, setting you up to better handle the other parts of the game you have left.
