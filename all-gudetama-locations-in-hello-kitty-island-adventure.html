    Ah yes, collectibles. While most games aren’t able to pull them off successfully, other games like Hello Kitty Island Adventure find a way to make collecting seemingly useless objects strangely fun.

    Recommended Videos

    Such is the case with Gudetamas, sleepy little egg creatures that players have to take pictures with. The only thing is that with a total of 96 Gudetama Locations to go through in what many call the best Hello Kitty game, it’s only natural that some players would struggle to, as a certain 12-year-old would say, “Catch ’em All.”

    Every Gudetama Location in Hello Kitty Island Adventure

    To make things easier for you guys, we’re here with a complete list of every single Gudetama in Hello Kitty Island Adventure. To further simplify things, we’ve also categorized every Gudetama with the area it’s found in.

    Before you start, it’s worth noting that two skills that will be valuable to master before you start your search for these lazy eggs are swimming and scuba diving. 

    Additionally, if you’re having difficulties keeping track of how many Gudetamas you have, then you can always interact with a Gudetama sign to check your progress. The easiest to reach sign is at the Seaside Resort, next to Pochacco.

    What Do You Get For Taking Pictures With Gudetamas?

    Every seasoned gamer knows that the one thing that makes collecting collectibles worth it is an awesome reward. Well, while players do indeed get rewarded for taking pictures with Gudetamas, not everyone will consider it to be worth it.

    For collecting Gudetamas, players get issues of The Great Gudetama Comic Book. You start with the ninth issue and work your way down as you obtain an issue every time you collect pictures of 12 Gudetamas.

    What Are Tophat Gudetamas Hello Kitty Island Adventure?

    Tophat Gudetamas are special Gudetamas that aren’t in the game from the start like the regular ones. Every week a new Gudetama appears in a randomized location, and it’s your job to find it and take a picture of it.

    Every new week, Pochacco will come to you and talk about how a new Tophat Gudetama is available to be found, and you can take that as your sign to go out and search for it.

    Fortunately, Tophat Gudetamas are significantly easier to find when compared to regular ones thanks to their purple and blue hats.
