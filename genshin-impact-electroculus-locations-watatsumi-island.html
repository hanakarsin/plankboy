    Genshin Impact Electroculus Locations: Watatsumi Island

    Genshin Impacts version 2.1 brought two new regions to the game, Watatsumi Island and Serai Island, both featuring new Electroculus, quests, treasure chests, challenges, and of course new enemies. With that said, we will now show you where to find all the Electroculus present in Watatsumi Island.

    09 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Genshin Impact‘s version 2.1 brought two new regions to the game, Watatsumi Island and Serai Island, both featuring new Electroculus, quests, treasure chests, challenges, and of course new enemies. With that said, we will now show you where to find all the Electroculus present in Watatsumi Island.

    How to get to Watatsumi Island

    If you are new to Inazuma, don’t worry, even if you will be guided to the Island as part of one of the region’s Archon Quests, you can get there sooner by heading west of Fort Mumei and then gliding or sailing your Waverider there. You can also freeze your way up there. The region features 21 of the 161 Eletroculus currently in the game and for our luck, differently than on Serai Island, you don’t need to deal with constant lightning to get them. To unlock the region’s map you need to, as always, first find the Statue of the Seven located there. To explore the region, we advise that you make use of Electro Characters such as Kujou Sara, Keqing, and the Raiden Shogun.

    All Electroculus Locations on Watatsumi Island

    To be able to get all Electroculus on the Island you will need to have raided your Sacred Sakura Favor level to at least 17 (Electrograna Lvl. 6) so that you can pass through all the barriers present there. You will also have to have finished The Moon-Bathed Deep World side-quest, which you can trigger by going to Watatsumi Island, more specifically at Mouun Shrine. To trigger the quest, you only need to talk to an NPC named Tsuyuko. Other than that, you won’t need to worry about any more adjacent quests. 

    You can check out where to find each Electroculus in the map below, courtesy of Genshin Impact’s official Interactive Map.

    Genshin Impact is currently available for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, mobile devices, and PC.
